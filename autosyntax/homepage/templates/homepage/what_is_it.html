What is it?

autosyntax turns simple words into python code.


For example:
list milk eggs nutella
becomes >
[ 'milk' ,  'eggs' ,  'nutella' ]



similarily,
dict meaning 42 happy pizza
becomes >
{ 'meaning' : 42 , 'happiness' : 'pizza' }







It can also do more complicated stuff,
like handling classes:

class Dog Animal init fluffy True
turns into >
class  Dog(Animal):
   def  __init__  ( self ,  fluffy= True ):
     self .fluffy = fluffy
     super (). __init__ ()

check out the inheritance, the __init__ declaration and arguments, the automatic self.fluffy datamember creation, super() etc.







it needs only 1 global hotkey to do everything,
but it's also flexible.

Variables.
Given:
buzz = 'lightyear'
then
words = tuple foo bar . buzz
will generate >
words = ( 'foo' , 'bar' , buzz)
thanks to the . preceding "buzz".



Inline methods:
for range numbers
becomes >
for number in range (numbers):
