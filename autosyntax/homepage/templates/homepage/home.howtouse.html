Basic principles

There are only 4 guiding principles:
{% br 1 %}
  • Merely words and spaces usually does the job.
    This yields the most common and likely use-case.
  • An Operator must appear before the arguments
    (e.g list , def , class  and so on).
  • An argument can be marked with a dot.
    This toggles a specific behaviour on/off.
  • An argument can be preceded by a built-in method.
    It is then passed to this method.
